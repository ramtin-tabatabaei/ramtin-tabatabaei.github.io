## Overview

This research examines how repeated robot failures affect user trust in collaborative tasks. By comparing homogeneous and heterogeneous failure sequences and assessing robots' awareness levels, the study provides insights into designing resilient human-robot interactions.

## Key Contributions

- Identified the impact of homogeneous vs. heterogeneous failure sequences on trust.
- Demonstrated the importance of robot awareness in maintaining trust and perceived intelligence.
- Highlighted failure severity's role in shaping user perception.
- Provided design insights for improving resilience in human-robot interactions.

## Method and System

A collaborative Tangram puzzle task was used to evaluate 54 participants' responses to varied robot failures. The experimental design manipulated failure type sequence (homogeneous vs. heterogeneous) and robot awareness levels (none, partial, full).

## Findings

1. Homogeneous failure sequences cause less trust decline than heterogeneous sequences.
2. Robots with partial or full awareness receive higher trust ratings.
3. The severity of failures influences trust and perceived intelligence.

## Limitations and Future Work

The study's controlled environment may not fully represent real-world dynamics where task complexity and stakes are higher. Future research could explore adaptive strategies and consider higher-stakes settings.

Future research could investigate adaptive repair strategies in real-world environments, examine longer-term interactions, and explore multi-robot or multi-human collaborations.
