abstract: "Robots are prone to errors, impacting their credibility in human-robot collaboration. This study analyses human gaze dynamics as signals for robot failures, categorized as executional and decisional. A study with 27 participants solving tangram puzzles revealed that the type and timing of failures significantly affect gaze behaviour and perception. Executional failures led to increased gaze shifts toward the robot, while decisional failures resulted in less variability in gaze transitions, especially when occurring at task end. Gaze can thus reliably indicate failures and suggest recovery actions."

<section class="paper-showcase__panel paper-showcase__panel--overview">
  <h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The research investigates the relationship between human gaze behaviours and robot failures. By conducting a study with robot-assisted tangram puzzles, the team explored how different failure types affect human perception and gaze dynamics, presenting gaze as a potential indicator for robot error detection and recovery.</p>
</section>

<section class="paper-showcase__panel paper-showcase__panel--contributions">
  <h2>Key Contributions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Identified gaze patterns as indicators of robot failures.</li>
    <li>Demonstrated impact of failure types on human perception and gaze.</li>
    <li>Highlighted timing effects of failures on gaze distribution.</li>
    <li>Showed acknowledgment of failures influences perception.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="paper-showcase__panel paper-showcase__panel--method">
  <h2>Method and System</h2>
<p>Participants worked with robots on tangram puzzles, experiencing both executional and decisional failures at different task stages, with varied acknowledgment from the robot.</p>
</section>

<section class="paper-showcase__panel paper-showcase__panel--results">
  <h2>Results Overview</h2>
<p>- Executional failures caused more gaze shifts toward robots.
- Decisional failures resulted in less variability in gaze transitions.
- The timing of failures affected gaze distribution.
- Gaze reliably indicates different types of robot failures.</p>
</section>

<section class="paper-showcase__panel paper-showcase__panel--discussion">
  <h2>Discussion</h2>
<h3>Limitations</h3>
<p>The study involved a limited number of participants and types of failures, restricting the generalizability of the findings.</p>
<h3>Future Work</h3>
<p>Future research could explore more diverse failure types and investigate other non-verbal cues that might indicate robotic failures.</p>
